Stop 1: Lourdes Day Care Centre

Lourdes Day Care Centre was founded in September 1979 and was officially opened on 8th December 1979. The idea for the Lourdes Day Care Centre began in the sacristy of Lourdes Church on Se√°n Mc Dermott Street Dublin 1. In the 1970's a group of local people saw that there was a need for a social centre for the elderly to have somewhere warm to meet and have a hot meal. Today in 2014, approximately 178 people access the Centre. The centre provides meals on wheels and dining room meals. It also provides arts, culture and heritage partnership projects for clients. Most of the activities in the Centre are aimed at people having fun; they strongly feel that laughter is the best medicine.

Stop 2: Larkin Community College

Larkin Commmunity College (LCC) is a secondary school in the heart of Dublin. The school promotes partnership with members of the community, working on projects that improve the area. This app is the product of one of these partnerships.


"1913 Lockout Tapestry"

Jim Larkin, after whom our school is named, was a worker's rights activist. On the 100 year anniversary of the Dublin Lockout, a giant tapestry was made to commemorate his achievements.

Stop 3: James Larkin

James "Big Jim" Larkin was a worker's rights activist. An imposing figure with a powerful voice, he led unfairly treated workers to revolt against their bosses and form trade unions, resulting in the 1913 Lockout.
